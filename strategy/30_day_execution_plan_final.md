## EDENA Governance Standing Orders (All 30 Days)

These are not reminders. They are standing orders. They apply to every asset, every day.

**GREEN content** (educational, framework, community — no PHI, no institutional data) may be drafted by May and published after a brief brand-alignment review. No delay required.

**YELLOW content** (policy claims, clinical guidance references, institutional positioning, AI-generated media, any content featuring Robert's personal clinical experience) requires Robert's review and explicit approval before publication. May prepares the draft; Robert approves. No Yellow content publishes without a human signature.

**RED content** does not exist in this plan. If any asset approaches RED territory — PHI exposure risk, unverified clinical claims, unapproved institutional data — it is blocked immediately and escalated to Robert. No override. No exceptions.

All AI-generated media carries a clear provenance disclosure. No deceptive content tactics. No astroturfing.

## The Flywheel Diagram (Narrative)

The three lanes are not independent. They are a flywheel. FMN content (Lane 1) builds an audience of nurses, nurse leaders, and healthcare executives. That audience encounters the Module 1 education offering (Lane 2) and the NINE² services page (Lane 3). Some become students. Some become clients. The students and clients generate testimonials and case studies. The testimonials and case studies become FMN content. The FMN content reaches a larger audience. The flywheel accelerates.

The first 30 days are about getting the flywheel moving. It will feel slow and heavy at first. That is normal. By Day 21, it will be moving on its own. By Day 30, it will be difficult to stop.

**"Stop charting, start treating."** Every day that Module 1 is not live is a day a nurse is not learning. Every day that the NINE² page is not live is a day a client is not finding you. Stop planning. Start treating.
