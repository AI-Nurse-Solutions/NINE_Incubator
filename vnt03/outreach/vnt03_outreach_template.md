## 1. Purpose

This document contains the email template for conducting validation interviews with potential nurse venture founders. This is the direct execution of the approved **Gate Card GC-001**. The goal of this outreach is to validate the core problem hypothesis of the VNT-03 (Nurse Venture Builder) track: that entrepreneurial nurses need a governed, structured, and accelerated pathway to build AI-assisted ventures.

## 2. Email Template

**Subject: Exploring a new model for nurse-led AI ventures**

Hi [Nurse Founder Name],

My name is Robert Domondon, and I'm the founder of the Nurse Intelligence Network (NIN). I'm reaching out because your work in [mention their specific area, e.g., clinical innovation, nursing leadership] suggests you see the same future we do: one where nurses are not just users of AI, but the architects and governors of it.

We are currently developing a new, structured incubator track—the **Nurse Venture Builder**—designed specifically to help entrepreneurial nurses build, govern, and scale their own AI-assisted ventures. The entire model is built on a "nurse-first, governance-always" framework called NAIO (Nursing AI Orchestration).

Before we build out the full program, we are doing the most important thing: talking to the nurses who would actually use it. 

Would you be open to a brief 20-minute conversation in the next week or two? I'm not selling anything. I'm looking to validate our core assumptions and ensure what we're building truly serves the needs of nurse founders like you. Specifically, I'd love to get your perspective on:

*   The biggest barriers you've seen for nurses trying to launch a tech-enabled idea.
*   Your thoughts on the concept of a formal "governance framework" for nurse-led AI.
*   What an ideal support structure for a nurse founder would look like to you.

Your insights would be incredibly valuable in shaping the direction of this work.

Thank you for your time and for the work you do.

Best,

Robert Elvin Domondon, CCRN, CSC, CMC, MD
Founder, Nurse Intelligence Network (NIN) & NAIO Institute
