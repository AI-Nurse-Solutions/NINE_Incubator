## 1. Purpose

The NAIO (Nursing AI Orchestration) Agent Registry is a core governance artifact. It is a machine-readable manifest that formally declares every autonomous agent operating within a NINE² niche track, linking each agent to its version, its function, and, most importantly, its designated human **NAIO Governance Anchor** (the steward). 

This registry serves three critical functions:
1.  **Accountability:** It provides a clear, auditable chain of command from every AI agent to a named, credentialed nurse.
2.  **Transparency:** It allows external parties (health systems, regulators, partners) to inspect the governance structure of a niche track and verify that every automated component is under human oversight.
3.  **Orchestration:** The Florence-X Conductor Engine uses this registry to route escalations from the Escalation Handler agent to the correct steward.

## 3. Management and Update Process

- **Generation:** The initial `agent-registry.json` is generated by the `fork-track.sh` script.
- **Updates:** The registry is updated automatically by the CI/CD pipeline whenever:
    - A new agent is added to the `cohort-config.yaml`.
    - An agent's version is incremented.
    - The `governance_anchor` is changed.
- **Auditing:** A copy of the registry is committed to the append-only audit log upon every change, providing a complete historical record of agent deployments and steward assignments.

This registry transforms governance from a policy document into a live, auditable system. It is the definitive record of who (which human) is accountable for what (which AI action), and it is the technical foundation for building institutional trust.
