## 1. Research Focus

Pricing model innovations in clinical AI SaaS. This pulse aimed to validate the proposed NINE² pricing model against current market trends and identify best practices for outcome-based pricing in a healthcare context.

## 2. Key Findings

| Finding | Implication for VNT-03 | Confidence | Source(s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shift to Outcome-Based Pricing is Real:** The SaaS industry, supercharged by AI, is broadly moving away from simple per-seat licenses toward usage- and outcome-based models. Customers want to pay for the value they receive, not just for access to the software. | The NINE² model (cohort fee + outcome-based equity) is highly aligned with this macro trend. It positions the incubator as a modern, value-aligned partner rather than a traditional service provider. | HIGH | [1] [2] [3] |

## 3. Summary

The research strongly validates the proposed two-stage pricing model. The broader market is moving toward outcome-based pricing, and the healthcare industry is deeply invested in the philosophy of value-based care. The NINE² model sits at the intersection of these two powerful trends.

The key is the clear, simple, and verifiable trigger for the outcome-based fee. While other SaaS companies might struggle to define and measure the specific "value" they provide, the NINE² model uses a major business milestone as a proxy for value creation, which is a robust and defensible approach.

The model is innovative, aligned with industry trends, and speaks the language of our target institutional clients. It is a significant competitive advantage.
