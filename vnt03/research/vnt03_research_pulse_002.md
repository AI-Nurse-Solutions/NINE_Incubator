## 1. Research Focus

Healthcare AI venture funding and acquisition patterns. This pulse aimed to understand the capital flow and exit landscape for companies in the clinical AI space to inform the VNT-03 track's long-term strategy.

## 2. Key Findings

| Finding | Implication for VNT-03 | Confidence | Source(s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **AI is Dominating Investment:** In 2025, AI-enabled companies captured 50-63% of all digital health funding, a massive increase from prior years. Total funding reached ~$14.2B. This is not a niche; it is the market. | VNT-03's focus on "AI-assisted" ventures is perfectly aligned with investor priorities. Ventures that do not have a credible AI and governance story will be at a significant fundraising disadvantage. | HIGH | [1] [2] [3] |
| **Shift to Provider Operations:** The largest share of funding (44%) is now going to solutions that improve provider operations (both administrative and clinical workflows), surpassing alternative care models. | This is the sweet spot for nurse-led ventures. Nurses are the experts in clinical workflow. VNT-03 should prioritize ventures that solve concrete, high-friction problems within existing health systems, as this is where capital is flowing. | HIGH | [4] |
| **M&A Activity is High and Strategic:** M&A deals jumped 61% in 2025. Acquirers are often larger digital health companies or established players like Thermo Fisher, and they are buying specific capabilities like "care coordination" or "hospital infrastructure." | This suggests a clear exit path for successful VNT-03 ventures. Instead of aiming for an IPO, a more viable strategy is to build a best-in-class, well-governed solution for a specific nursing workflow and plan for a strategic acquisition by a larger platform company. | HIGH | [5] [6] |

## 3. Summary

The capital market has unequivocally validated the core premise of NINE². Investors are pouring billions into AI-driven solutions that solve the operational and clinical workflow challenges of healthcare providers. The era of generic wellness apps is being supplanted by a focus on high-acuity, high-impact tools that integrate into the existing healthcare stack.

Furthermore, the active M&A market provides a clear and attainable exit strategy for nurse-led ventures. The goal for a VNT-03 company should be to become an attractive acquisition target by demonstrating a well-governed, highly effective solution to a painful, specific nursing problem. This aligns perfectly with the NINE² doctrine of focusing on the "anchor workflow."
