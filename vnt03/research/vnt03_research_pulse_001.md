## 1. Research Focus

Emerging nurse entrepreneurship models and market gaps. This pulse aimed to validate the fundamental premise of the VNT-03 (Nurse Venture Builder) track by assessing the size, funding landscape, and maturity of the nurse-led venture ecosystem.

## 2. Key Findings

| Finding | Implication for VNT-03 | Confidence | Source(s) |

| **Market Size is Substantial but Undefined:** Estimates suggest 0.5% to 1% of all nurses identify as entrepreneurs. With over 4 million nurses in the US, this represents a potential market of 20,000-40,000 individuals. However, the definition is broad, covering everything from solo consultants to tech founders. | The market is large enough to support a dedicated incubator. ## 3. Summary

The nurse entrepreneurship market is in a crucial transition phase. It is moving from an informal collection of individual practitioners to a formal, funded, and increasingly tech-focused ecosystem. The market is large, the funding infrastructure is beginning to form, and the competitive landscape is open.

This is a strategically ideal time to launch the VNT-03 Nurse Venture Builder. The focus on governance, orchestration, and institutional trust directly addresses the maturity gap in the current market and positions cohort ventures for premium valuation and accelerated adoption.
